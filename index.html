Welcome

Welcome to the Hulmeville Borough website. This website is intended to provide our residents and visitors with the latest information on the borough operation, emergency and non-emergency public services, meetings and events, along with information on how to contact us. This website is, and always will be, a work in progress in order to bring the most current and useful information to you.

We believe that Hulmeville Borough is a wonderful place to live, work, and raise a family. It is the intention of the Mayor and Borough Council to provide professional, efficient, timely, and fiscally responsible services in order to continue to support this quality of life.

Please enjoy browsing through our website.

We would enjoy hearing from you, with comments or questions about either our website or the Borough. Please contact us with any comments, questions, feedback and/or suggestions.

REMINDER! Notice of parking ordinance restricting parking during snow conditions

In accordance with the Borough snow events ordinance, everyone is reminded that parking on Borough streets is prohibited from 1 hour after snow begins to fall and until 12 hours after the snow ceases to fall. Anyone in violation of the ordinance is subject to fines of up to $300.

Trash pickup reminder

As a reminder, Leck will only pickup ordinary household waste.  Some guidelines are:

  • Residential waste, or standard household waste, includes food waste, rags, old clothes, leather, rubber, glass and crockery, and other household items<
  • A maximum of 4 trash containers, not weighing more than 50 lbs each, are allowed
  • Construction debris/buildng materials, hazardous materials, or electronics are not allowed
  • One bulk item is allowed per week

For more information refer to Leck's informational brochure

Posted 2020-11-01
Rewriting Hulmeville's History - Upcoming book for the Borough Sesquicentennial(2022)

In 2022 Hulmeville will celebrate the 150th anniversary of its incorporation as an independent borough. Prior to 1872, Hulmeville was an informal village that was part of Middletown Township. As part of our plans to celebrate the sesquicentennial, we are working on updating the written History of Hulmeville. A well-documented history was written in 1972 as Hulmeville celebrated its 100th anniversary but with updated research tools we hope to expand on this history of the first hundred years and add some of the major developments of the subsequent fifty years. While this is a work in progress, periodically over the coming months we will add some stories from the "new" history of Hulmeville to the website. These new stories, while in draft form, will be a part of the revised history that will be published and for sale in 2022.

Go to the History page to read short essays from the upcoming book.
